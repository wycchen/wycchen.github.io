Summary

Cryptography is the science of designing algorithms and protocols that guarantee privacy, authenticity, and integrity of data when parties are communicating or computing in an insecure environment. The recent explosion of electronic communication and commerce has expanded the significance of cryptography far beyond its historical military role into all of our daily lives. In particular, cryptography provides the technology for realizing different kinds of security; for example, it allows us to use our credit card to make online purchases without revealing the credit card number to other people.

The past 25 years have also seen cryptography transformed from an ad hoc collection of mysterious tricks into a rigorous science based on firm complexity-theoretic foundations. In this course, we will focus on the following directions of cryptography.
1. Definitions: It is important to precisely define cryptographic problems, and show how to do for some cryptographic problems.
2. Constructions and Proofs of Security: Introduce examples of general and concrete solutions to various cryptographic problems, and prove that they satisfy the definitions mentioned above based on hardness assumptions.
3. Foundations: The hardness assumptions are always used in build the constructions. We will introduce some well-known assumptions and discuss its availability in theory and practice.
4. Applications: We will see some examples of how to address cryptographic issues in higher-level protocol problems.
5. Information Security: Use cryptographic constructions as a black box to realize network and multimedia security.
